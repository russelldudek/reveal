Learn before standardizing.

A discovery-led plan for a confidential global enterprise.

Entry principle: Do not begin with a technology roadmap. Begin with executive intent, the current portfolio, one representative workflow, decision rights, architecture reality, and the evidence needed to make the next investment decisions.
0–30

Read the system

Listen for

  • Executive priorities and business-unit demand
  • Current AI portfolio, pilots, platforms, and shadow activity
  • Data, integration, identity, cloud, security, privacy, legal
  • Team topology, vendor commitments, capacity, and decision rights

Produce

  • Portfolio reality map
  • Stakeholder and authority topology
  • Architecture and governance baseline
  • One representative workflow baseline
  • Immediate risk / stop-work list only where necessary
31–60

Make decisions visible

Establish

  • Enterprise AI intake and prioritization standard
  • Fund / condition / narrow / hold / stop vocabulary
  • Risk tiering and early review paths
  • Build-vs-buy and exception decision records

Pilot the operating model

  • Review two representative initiatives
  • Map value, readiness, authority, architecture, adoption
  • Set outcome and leading-indicator scorecard
  • Confirm production ownership and review cadence

61–90

Deliver one scalable increment

Solution delivery

  • Confirm reference architecture and exception rationale
  • Define evaluation, acceptance, telemetry, cost, rollback
  • Make human authority and escalation explicit
  • Align delivery roles across engineers, architects, administrators, business owners, and vendors

Workflow embedding

  • Redesign roles and standard work
  • Prepare training and champion support
  • Instrument retained use, friction, overrides, and task success
  • Run a controlled release and rapid learning cadence

Operating Cadence

CadencePurposeOutput
Weekly delivery and risk reviewResolve blockers, evidence gaps, ownership, and decisionsUpdated conditions and escalations
Biweekly portfolio reviewSequence demand against capacity and readinessFund / condition / hold / stop decisions
Monthly architecture and governance learningConvert repeated patterns and exceptions into standardsReference pattern or revised guardrail
Monthly value and adoption reviewCompare expected vs retained value in real workScale, revise, or retire recommendation

What I Would Not Do

No platform-first roadmap

Do not assume the tool portfolio defines the business portfolio.

No governance theater

Do not add review steps that cannot change a decision.

No pilot accumulation

Do not leave experiments alive because stopping feels politically difficult.

No adoption appendix

Do not treat changed work as a communications task after delivery.

Proof objective by day 90: one initiative has moved through an explicit yield system with accountable ownership, architecture and controls, accepted delivery, workflow adoption, instrumentation, and a decision on what to scale or change.
91–120

Institutionalize enterprise AI yield

Publish

  • Enterprise AI strategy and sequenced roadmap
  • Portfolio intake and prioritization operating standard
  • Reference architecture and exception ownership
  • Responsible-AI and human-authority model
  • Executive scorecard and reporting cadence

Confirm

  • Team accountabilities and capability gaps
  • Vendor roles, economics, and knowledge transfer
  • Business-unit ownership and champion network
  • Operating support and lifecycle management
  • Scale, hold, and retirement decisions

Discovery Risks to Validate

Demand risk

Executive urgency may bypass qualification and capacity.

Architecture risk

Multiple providers and local solutions may create hidden integration and identity debt.

Ownership risk

Business sponsorship may not translate into workflow and lifecycle accountability.

Adoption risk

Success may be counted at go-live rather than retained behavior and outcome.
